# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 2026-06-25

Snippetry is a macOS snippet manager that stores your snippets, tags, and folders on your own device and in your own iCloud account. This page explains, in plain language, what that means for your data.

## Summary

- Snippetry **does not collect, transmit, or have access to any of your data.**
- The developer cannot see your snippets, their titles, your tags, or anything else you store in the app.
- There are no analytics, no tracking, no third-party SDKs, and no advertising.

## What Snippetry stores

The app stores the content you create: snippets (titles, code, language, notes), tags, and folders. All of this lives on your Mac, and is synced to your personal iCloud account using Apple's CloudKit private database if you are signed into iCloud and have iCloud Drive enabled for Snippetry.

## Where your data lives

- **On your Mac:** in the app's local SwiftData store, inside the app's sandbox container.
- **In your iCloud account:** in the private CloudKit database belonging to your Apple ID. Apple's terms govern this storage; the Snippetry developer has no access to it.

The app's only outbound network use is talking to Apple's CloudKit servers to sync your private data between your own devices.

## Third parties

Snippetry does not use third-party analytics, telemetry, advertising, crash reporting, or any other third-party data services. The only external service involved is **Apple iCloud**, and only with your own Apple ID.

## Children's privacy

Snippetry does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children under 13.

## Changes to this policy

If this policy changes in any meaningful way, the effective date above will be updated, and the change will be reflected in this document on GitHub so the history is public.
